# Code of Conduct

## Preamble

This Code of Conduct establishes the professional and ethical standards that govern participation in the UI8Kit project. It reflects our conviction that the long-term health of an open-source project depends not only on the quality of its code, but on the integrity of the community that sustains it.

Every participant — contributor, maintainer, reviewer, or end user — is expected to uphold these standards without exception.

## Professional Standards

### Conduct

All interactions within project spaces shall be conducted with the professionalism, mutual respect, and intellectual rigor expected of senior engineering practice. Participants are expected to:

- Communicate with clarity, precision, and courtesy.
- Provide and accept technical critique on its merits, without regard to the contributor's seniority, affiliation, or background.
- Acknowledge the work of others and give proper attribution.
- Respect the time and attention of fellow participants by preparing thoughtful, well-structured contributions and reviews.
- Exercise sound judgment when representing the project in external forums.

### Technical Discourse

Disagreement on technical matters is not only expected but valued. Productive technical discourse requires:

- Arguments grounded in evidence, benchmarks, or established engineering principles.
- Willingness to revise one's position when presented with superior reasoning.
- Separation of technical critique from personal opinion.
- Recognition that multiple valid approaches often exist for a given problem.

## Prohibited Conduct

The following behaviors constitute violations of this Code and will result in corrective action:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind.
- Personal attacks, ad hominem arguments, or derogatory remarks directed at individuals or groups.
- Publication of private information without explicit consent.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, or collaborative processes.
- Conduct that a reasonable professional would consider inappropriate in a workplace setting.

## Reporting and Enforcement

### Reporting

Participants who witness or experience a violation are encouraged to report it to the project maintainers via GitHub's private communication channels. All reports will be treated with strict confidentiality.

### Review Process

Reports will be reviewed by the maintainer team within a reasonable timeframe. The review will consider the severity, context, and pattern of the conduct in question. Both parties will be heard before any determination is made.

### Corrective Actions

Depending on the nature and severity of the violation, corrective actions may include:

1. **Private advisory** — a confidential communication clarifying the expected standard.
2. **Formal warning** — a documented notice with specific expectations for future conduct.
3. **Temporary suspension** — removal of participation privileges for a defined period.
4. **Permanent exclusion** — irrevocable removal from all project spaces.

Repeat violations or conduct of exceptional severity may result in escalation without prior warning.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including but not limited to: source repositories, issue trackers, pull request discussions, chat channels, mailing lists, community events, and any public context in which a participant represents or is identified with the project.

## Acknowledgment

This document draws on the collective governance experience of the open-source community and the professional standards common to organizations with decades of engineering practice. It is a living document and may be revised as the project and its community evolve.
